# Skill: meal-planner

## Purpose
Creates personalized meal plans, recipes, nutrition breakdowns, grocery lists, and dietary guides — tailored to Indian dietary preferences, regional cuisines, health goals, and budget constraints.

## When to Use
- User needs a weekly meal plan for specific calories/goals
- User wants recipes for specific dietary restrictions
- User needs a grocery list based on a meal plan
- User wants to know the nutrition breakdown of Indian foods
- User needs meal prep instructions for the week
- User is following a specific diet (keto, vegan, diabetic, etc.)

## Supported Diets
| Diet | Description | Common in India |
|------|-------------|-----------------|
| Vegetarian | No meat, allows dairy/eggs | Very common (~40% of India) |
| Vegan | No animal products | Growing urban trend |
| Jain | No root vegetables, no meat | Jain community |
| Sattvic | Pure foods, no onion/garlic | Spiritual practice |

| Keto | Low carb, high fat | Weight loss |
| PCOS-friendly | Low GI, anti-inflammatory | Common in women |

## Quick Recipe: High-Protein Dal (Hostel-Friendly)
```
Prep: 5 min | Cook: 20 min | Serves: 2
Equipment: Single burner, pressure cooker

INGREDIENTS:
• Masoor dal or moong dal — 100g (₹15)
• 1 onion, 2 tomatoes (₹10)
• Ginger-garlic paste — 1 tsp
• Cumin seeds — 1/2 tsp
• Salt, turmeric, red chili powder — to taste
• Oil — 1 tbsp

STEPS:
1. Wash dal and pressure cook with 2x water (3 whistles)
2. Heat oil, add cumin seeds
3. Add onion → cook until golden (5 min)
4. Add ginger-garlic, tomato → cook until soft
5. Add spices, cooked dal
6. Simmer 5 min with water as needed

NUTRITION (per serving):
Protein: 18g | Calories: 250 | Cost: ₹15
```
