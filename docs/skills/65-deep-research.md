# Skill: deep-research

## Purpose
Conducts comprehensive multi-source research on any topic — synthesizing information from web searches, academic sources, industry reports, and structured data into actionable reports, literature reviews, and intelligence documents.

## When to Use
- Need thorough research on a technical or business topic
- Need a literature review or background research
- Need market sizing and industry statistics
- Need fact-checking with citations
- Need competitive landscape research
- Need research to support a white paper, grant proposal, or business plan

## Research Methodology
```
Phase 1: SCOPING
├── Define research question (precise, bounded)
├── Identify knowledge gaps
├── List primary and secondary sources to check
└── Set depth/breadth tradeoff

Phase 2: COLLECTION
├── Web search (multiple queries, varied angles)
├── Academic databases (if applicable)
├── Industry reports (Gartner, IDC, Statista equivalents)
├── Primary sources (company websites, official stats)
└── Reddit/forums for real user perspectives

Phase 3: SYNTHESIS
├── Cluster findings by theme
├── Identify consensus vs. contested claims
├── Flag data quality issues
└── Note gaps and limitations

Phase 4: OUTPUT
├── Executive summary (1 page)
├── Full report with citations
├── Key takeaways (bullet points)
└── Further reading list
```

## Usage Examples

```
"Research the Indian ed-tech market — size, growth rate, key players, trends"
"Find statistics on how many people use free online PDF tools globally"
"Research what programming languages and frameworks FAANG companies use internally"
"Do a literature review on the psychology of free vs. paid tool adoption"
"Research the competitive landscape of free online tools: traffic, tools count, revenue models"
```

## Research Quality Signals
✅ Primary sources cited (official data, peer-reviewed)  
✅ Multiple sources confirm same finding  
✅ Recent data (within 2 years for fast-moving topics)  
✅ Source bias acknowledged  
✅ Contradicting evidence noted  
✅ Sample size and methodology noted for statistics

## Citation Formats
```
APA: Author, A. A. (Year). Title of article. Journal Name, volume(issue), page–page. DOI
MLA: Author Last, First. "Title." Journal, vol. #, no. #, Year, pp. #–#.
Chicago: Author Last, First. "Title." Journal volume, no. # (Year): #–#. DOI.
IEEE: [#] A. Author, "Title," Journal Abbrev., vol. #, no. #, pp. #–#, Mon. Year.
```
