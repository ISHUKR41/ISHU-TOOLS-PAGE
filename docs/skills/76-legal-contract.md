# Skill: legal-contract

## Purpose
Generates legal document templates — contracts, agreements, NDAs, terms of service, privacy policies, MOUs, employment letters, and more — tailored for Indian and international legal contexts. All outputs include disclaimers to consult a qualified lawyer.

## When to Use
- Freelancer needs a client service agreement
- Startup needs Terms of Service and Privacy Policy
- Need an NDA for a business conversation
- Need an employment/internship offer letter
- Need a rental/lease agreement
- Need a partnership or co-founder agreement
- Need a user consent form or data processing agreement

## Document Types

### 1. Service Agreement (Freelance)
```
Key Clauses:
✅ Scope of work (detailed deliverables)
✅ Payment terms and schedule
✅ Revision policy (how many rounds included)
✅ Intellectual property ownership
✅ Confidentiality clause
✅ Termination conditions
✅ Dispute resolution (jurisdiction)
✅ Force majeure
✅ Governing law (state and country)
```

### 2. Non-Disclosure Agreement (NDA)
```
Types:
- Unilateral: One party discloses, other protects
- Mutual: Both parties exchange confidential info
- Multi-party: Three or more parties

Duration options:
- During engagement only
- 2 years post-engagement (standard)
- Perpetual (for trade secrets)
```

### 3. Terms of Service (SaaS/Website)
```
Required Sections:
1. Acceptance of terms
2. User eligibility and account creation
3. Acceptable use policy
4. Intellectual property
5. Limitation of liability
6. Disclaimer of warranties
7. Termination
8. Privacy policy reference
9. Changes to terms
10. Contact information and governing law
```

### 4. Privacy Policy (PDPB India compliant)
```
Required Disclosures:
- What personal data is collected
- Why it is collected (purpose)
- How it is stored and protected
- Who it is shared with (third parties)
- Data retention period
- User rights (access, correction, deletion)
- Cookie policy
- Contact for data-related requests
- Last updated date
```

## Indian Legal Framework Reference

### Key Acts
| Act | Relevance |
|-----|-----------|
| IT Act, 2000 (amended 2008) | E-commerce, cybercrime, digital signatures |
| PDPB 2023 / DPDP Act | Personal data protection |
| Consumer Protection Act 2019 | User rights for online services |
| Indian Contract Act 1872 | Validity of contracts |
| Copyright Act 1957 | IP in software and content |
| GST Act | Tax on services |

### Contract Validity Requirements (India)
```
Under Indian Contract Act 1872, a valid contract needs:
✅ Offer and acceptance
✅ Lawful consideration
✅ Capacity to contract (18+, sound mind)
✅ Free consent (no coercion, fraud, misrepresentation)
✅ Lawful object (not illegal or immoral)
✅ Not expressly declared void
```

```

Date: [Date]

Dear [Candidate Name],

SUBJECT: OFFER OF [INTERNSHIP/EMPLOYMENT] — [POSITION TITLE]

We are pleased to offer you the position of [Title] at [Company Name],
effective [Start Date].

COMPENSATION:
Monthly Stipend: ₹[Amount]
Performance Bonus: Up to ₹[Amount] based on milestones

DURATION:
[3/6/12] months, with option to extend based on performance

RESPONSIBILITIES:
[3-5 bullet points of key responsibilities]

TERMS:
• This is a [remote/on-site] position
• Working hours: [hours] per week
• Confidentiality: You agree not to disclose company information
• IP: All work created belongs to [Company Name]
• Notice period: [X] weeks by either party

Please sign and return this letter by [Date] to confirm acceptance.

Sincerely,
[Authorized Signatory]
[Company Name]
────────────────────────
ACCEPTANCE:
I, [Name], accept the above offer.
Signature: _________________  Date: ________________
```
